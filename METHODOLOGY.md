# Methodology

The current demo champion is an interpretable regularized ensemble. House gives greater weight to district baseline and national environment; Senate gives greater weight to candidate strength and incumbency. Polls, when genuinely ingested, receive recency/count-dependent weight. Margins map to probability with a normal uncertainty model; open, sparse-polling and redrawn races widen intervals. Ratings are labels over probabilities, never substitutes for them.

National shocks plus race shocks are simulated at least 50,000 times, retaining correlation. State-specific, ranked-choice, and runoff modules are registered as challengers until genuine out-of-sample evidence supports promotion. No demo output is a live forecast or claim of accuracy.
